When planning this trip the "wet season" is between December and May. This is the best time to visit because although it will be rainy and wet, the water and air temperatures will be warmer and there will be sunny skies and calm seas, as it only lightly rains occasionally.

Geography:

The Galapagos Islands are located on either side of the equator in the Eastern Pacific Ocean closest to Ecuador. They consist of 18 main islands, 3 small islands, and over 100 rocks. The 18 main islands are: Baltra Island, Bartolomé Island, Darwin Island, Española Island, Fernandina Island, Floreana Island, Genovesa Island, Isabela Island, Marchena Island, North Seymour Island, Pinzón Island, Pinta Island, Rábida Island, San Cristóbl Island, Santa Cruz Island, Santa Fe Island, Satiago Island, and Wolf Island. The three small islands are: Daphne Major, South Plaza Isand, and Nameless Island.

Wildlife:

As you may know the Galapagos Islands are home to many rare species discovered by the famous scientist, Charles Darwin. There are over 2,000 different species in the Galapagos rangning from fish to birds. Officials at the Galapagos National Park say there are 79 fish, 42 reptiles, 45 birds, 15 mammals, over 1,000 invertebrates, 271 plants, and 130 types of seaweed across all of the islands.

Basic Information Such as Transportation and Food:

The Galapagos is made up of many islands, so the question is "How do you travel from island to island?" Well, there are many options including: boats, water taxis, buses, ferry's, and land taxi's. What about the food? That is a simple question, there are many basic Ecuadorian meals. These meals consist of meat, potatoes, grains, fish, and seafood. There is something for everyone!
